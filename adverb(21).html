✯ Adverbs ✯

  1. ゆっくり (yukkuri) - slowly
  2. 速く (hayaku) - quickly
  3. 今 (ima) - now
  4. とても (totemo) - very
  5. よく (yoku) - well
  6. もう (mou) - already
  7. まだ (mada) - still/yet
  8. 大丈夫 (だいじょうぶ, daijoubu) - alright/okay
  9. 必ず (かならず, kanarazu) - surely/certainly
  10. ちょっと (chotto) - a little/bit
  11. すぐに (sugu ni) - immediately/right away
  12. もっと (motto) - more
  13. 全然 (ぜんぜん, zenzen) - not at all/completely
  14. だんだん (dandan) - gradually
  15. 頻繁に (ひんぱんに, hinpan ni) - frequently
  16. 一緒に (いっしょに, issho ni) - together
  17. 夜遅く (よるおそく, yoru osoku) - late at night
  18. たくさん (takusan) - a lot
  19. 実際に (じっさいに, jissai ni) - actually/in fact
  20. より (yori) - more/than

How to Use Adverbs in Japanese:

  1. Modifying Verbs: Adverbs can modify verbs to describe how an action is performed.
  2. Modifying Adjectives: They can also modify adjectives to intensify or specify the degree.
  3. Modifying Other Adverbs: Adverbs can modify other adverbs to express details about the manner or degree.
  4. Sentence Placement: Adverbs in Japanese can generally be placed before the verb they modify or at the beginning or end of a sentence for emphasis.
  5. Politeness and Context: Some adverbs like すぐに (sugu ni) and もう (mou) can convey urgency or completion, affecting the tone of the sentence.

Notes:

  • Adverbs in Japanese often end in に (ni) when modifying verbs or adjectives.
  • They play a crucial role in providing context, manner, frequency, and intensity in Japanese sentences.

Understanding how to use adverbs effectively will enhance your ability to communicate nuances and details in Japanese conversation and writing.
