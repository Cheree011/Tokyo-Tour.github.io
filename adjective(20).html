Japanese Adjectives

い-adjectives (い形容詞)

  • ♢ 新しい (あたらしい, atarashii) - new
  • ♢ 高い (たかい, takai) - high/tall
  • ♢ 広い (ひろい, hiroi) - wide/spacious
  • ♢ 古い (ふるい, furui) - old
  • ♢ 大きい (おおきい, ookii) - big/large
  • ♢ 小さい (ちいさい, chiisai) - small
  • ♢ 美しい (うつくしい, utsukushii) - beautiful
  • ♢ 強い (つよい, tsuyoi) - strong
  • ♢ 弱い (よわい, yowai) - weak
  • ♢ 熱い (あつい, atsui) - hot

Usage of い-adjectives: They directly precede nouns they describe. E.g., 新しい車 (あたらしいくるま, atarashii kuruma) - new car. They conjugate to show tense and formality like verbs. E.g., 高かった (たかかった, takakatta) - was high/tall.

な-adjectives (な形容詞)

  • ♢ 静か (しずか, shizuka) - quiet
  • ♢ 忙しい (いそがしい, isogashii) - busy
  • ♢ 簡単 (かんたん, kantan) - easy/simple
  • ♢ 便利 (べんり, benri) - convenient
  • ♢ 大切 (たいせつ, taisetsu) - important
  • ♢ 丁寧 (ていねい, teinei) - polite
  • ♢ 素晴らしい (すばらしい, subarashii) - wonderful
  • ♢ 面白い (おもしろい, omoshiroi) - interesting/funny
  • ♢ 元気 (げんき, genki) - energetic
  • ♢ 上手 (じょうず, jouzu) - skillful

Usage of な-adjectives: They are placed before nouns they modify. E.g., 静かな場所 (しずかなばしょ, shizuka na basho) - quiet place. In polite/formal contexts, they are followed by です. E.g., 簡単です (かんたんです, kantan desu) - It is easy/simple.

Comparison with English:

  • Similarities: Adjectives in Japanese and English both modify nouns and can express qualities.
  • Differences: Japanese adjectives can also act as predicates without needing copulas (like "is" in English).

Mastering adjectives is essential for expressing descriptions and qualities in Japanese sentences. Their usage varies depending on the context and formality level, similar to verbs.
