✯ Verbs ✯

  • ♢ 食べる (たべる, taberu) - to eat
  • ♢ 行く (いく, iku) - to go
  • ♢ 見る (みる, miru) - to see, to watch
  • ♢ 書く (かく, kaku) - to write
  • ♢ 聞く (きく, kiku) - to listen, to ask
  • ♢ 走る (はしる, hashiru) - to run
  • ♢ 泳ぐ (およぐ, oyogu) - to swim
  • ♢ 話す (はなす, hanasu) - to speak, to talk
  • ♢ 聞く (きく, kiku) - to hear, to ask (Note: Same dictionary form, but different kanji)
  • ♢ 買う (かう, kau) - to buy
  • ♢ 見せる (みせる, miseru) - to show
  • ♢ 来る (くる, kuru) - to come
  • ♢ 知る (しる, shiru) - to know
  • ♢ 使う (つかう, tsukau) - to use
  • ♢ 考える (かんがえる, kangaeru) - to think
  • ♢ 教える (おしえる, oshieru) - to teach, to tell
  • ♢ 知れる (しれる, shireru) - to be known, to find out
  • ♢ 歩く (あるく, aruku) - to walk
  • ♢ 着る (きる, kiru) - to wear, to put on

These are just a selection of verbs in their dictionary forms. Japanese verbs conjugate according to tense, mood, and formality level, so their endings will change depending on how they are used in a sentence.

Understanding these verbs and their conjugations is crucial for constructing sentences and expressing actions in Japanese.
